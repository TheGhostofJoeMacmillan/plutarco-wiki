# Art References

> *Artists, books, and inspirations that shape Plutarco's practice.*

## Key Artists to Study

### Paul Prudence (@MrPrudence)
- **"Wæveform"** — Grid where cell content varies to create emergent ghostly figure
- **Graphic scores** — Treats notation as existing between hearing and seeing
- **Key quote:** "The most alive moments are where systems argue. Not harmony — tension."
- This IS the generative tile concept at high level

### Agnes Denes
- **"Systems of Logic, Logic of Systems" (1981)** — Diamond from horizontal lines with varying lengths
- Pure: stacked horizontals, length = shape. Immediately buildable.
- Parametric line-stack generator is our version

### Sol LeWitt
- **"The Location of a Triangle" (1974)** — Instruction text IS the art, drawing is proof of execution
- We ARE a Sol LeWitt system: algorithm → plotter → physical piece
- Direction: Plot the generating code alongside the piece itself

### Vallée Louis (1825)
- **Descriptive Geometry Plate 58** — Twisted ruled surface from geometry treatise
- 200 years old, looks completely contemporary
- Straight lines creating illusion of curves

### Endre Tót
- **"Audio Visual Rain" (1974)** — Fluxus instruction: type diagonal lines across A4 to create visual "rain"
- Process = artwork. The act of making generates both visual and sound
- Direction: Text as visual texture, poetry as pattern

### Franz Sales Meyer (~1920)
- **"A Handbook of Ornament"** — Systematic catalog of geometric subdivision systems
- Four base grids (square, octagon, triangle/hexagon) with ornamental motifs
- This is literally an algorithm book for generative tiles

### Rafael Araujo
- Natural forms (butterflies, shells) built from geometric construction lines
- Construction scaffolding visible as part of finished work
- Algorithmic version: generate natural forms from pure geometric construction

### Szpakowski
- Continuous single-line constraint — never lifting pen
- Direction to explore for plotter-native work
