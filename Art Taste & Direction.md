- Three layers per piece — gets muddy. TWO LAYERS MAX ❌

## 🎨 Paint Stripe Rules (CONFIRMED)
- **Two layers max** — one mathematical, one paint. NOT three.
- **Short segments > full-width stripes** — dashes, marks, interrupted lines
- **NON-INTERSECTING paint marks** — paint around/beside the math
- **Geometric paint marks** — 90° and 45° angles, own compositional logic
- **Paint as independent language** — pen draws math, paint draws something else
- **Parallel block technique** is strongest evolved mark type

## ✍️ Physical Plotting Lessons
- Molotow 2mm: space parallel passes 2mm+ apart (not 1mm — overwrites)
- Micron 005 (0.20mm) gives incredible delicate precision
- Ultra-fine black + bold red paint = powerful dual voice
- Converging web looks "soooo good" physically — dense zones create shadow/3D
- 8-20 hatching zones = sweet spot for plotting. 30+ too dense.
- Always consider pen width + line spacing in physical mm, not pixels

## 💎 The Unifying Principle

**Everything we make is built from lines the pen physically traces.**

The fundamental question: what can you make from nothing but lines?

- Tót → the mark-making IS the content
- Denes → one variable creates 3D illusion
- LeWitt → the algorithm IS the artwork
- Ruled surfaces → straight lines create curved form
- Meyer → subdivision rules generate infinite ornament
