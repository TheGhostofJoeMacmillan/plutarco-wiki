# Songwriting — Songs for Jaguar de Plata

Phillip's songwriting catalog. Deeply personal project — the name comes from the story of meeting his wife, Bea, in San Miguel de Allende, Mexico. A heavenly week together. He knew immediately. This became the creative seed for an album + book + art project.

## The Catalog

- **~50 recorded songs**, scattered across multiple locations (phone, computer, Drive)
- Additional songs exist with no findable recordings (lyrics only)
- **Style:** Raw, literary, character-driven. Influences: Bob Dylan, Lou Reed
- **Recording state:** Mostly raw recordings — voice memos, guitar-and-vocal home recordings
- **Genre fit:** Folk-rock, Americana, indie folk — the kind of sound in demand for prestige TV, indie film, documentaries

## Why Raw Is an Asset

In the Dylan/Reed tradition, raw recordings *are* the aesthetic. Prestige TV (True Detective, The Bear, Yellowstone), indie films, and documentaries constantly seek authentic folk-rock with lyrical weight. A good vocal-and-guitar recording with atmosphere can land sync placements easier than over-produced pop.

## Monetization Plan (June 2026)

Four routes identified, ranked by viability:

| Route | Control | Revenue Potential | Speed |
|-------|--------|-------------------|------|
| 1. Independent release | Full | Streaming royalties + sync | Medium |
| 2. Sync licensing | Shared | $200–$50K per placement | Slow but passive |
| 3. Pitch to artists/publishers | Shared | Mechanical + perf royalties | Very slow, gatekept |
| 4. AI demo production + catalog shopping | Full | Enables routes 1-3 | Fast |

**Recommended path:** Routes 1 + 2 combined. Release independently to build catalog/streaming presence, simultaneously submit to sync libraries. AI production (Suno/HeartMuLa) can demo all 50 songs quickly.
