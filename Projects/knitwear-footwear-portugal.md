## Part 1: Knitwear Manufacturers

Portugal is a major European knitwear hub. The northern region (Barcelos, Guimarães, Braga, Famalicão) concentrates most production. Key strengths: circular knit, flat-bed knitting (Shima Seiki), fully-fashioned knitwear, jersey, fleece, and technical knits. Certifications commonly held: OEKO-TEX 100, GOTS, GRS, ISO 9001.

### Industry Context
- Portugal is the 5th largest textile producer in Europe, 3rd largest EU apparel exporter
- ~12,000 textile/clothing companies, 130,000+ workers
- Exported €5.5 billion in 2025
- 70%+ of production in the Ave Valley / Guimarães region
- Most factories within 45 min drive of Porto
- MOQs for knitwear typically 100-300 units/style (smaller factories go lower)
- Lead times: 4-8 weeks (vs 12-16 from Asia)
- August shutdown: 2-3 weeks mid-August — plan around it

## Part 2: Footwear Manufacturers

### Industry Context
- Portugal produces ~80 million pairs of shoes annually (4th largest shoe exporter in Europe by value)
- 150+ years of premium leather goods production
- Main cluster: **Felgueiras–Guimarães–Santo Tirso triangle** (30-50km NE of Porto) — "Shoe Valley"
- Felgueiras alone has 400+ registered footwear manufacturers
- Secondary cluster: **São João da Madeira** (automated, mid-market, higher volumes 5,000+)
- **Sweet spot:** Premium and contemporary leather footwear
- Women's leather shoes/boots = strongest segment
- Ecosystem: leather tanneries, sole manufacturers, lasting machines, eyelet suppliers, packaging — all within close driving distance
- Local tanning industry in Alcanena (~120km south of Porto)

### MOQ Reality Check

| Factory Size | MOQ per Style | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Large (200+ employees) | 500-1,000 pairs | Usually works with established wholesale brands |
| Mid-size (50-200 employees) | 150-400 pairs | Good fit for growing DTC brands |
| Small atelier (10-50 employees) | 50-150 pairs | Best for limited editions, early-stage brands |

**Key detail:** MOQs are often **per-colour, not per-style**. A boot in black and brown at 150-pair MOQ = 150 black + 150 brown = 300 total pairs.

### Pricing (Ex-Factory / FOB Porto)

| Product | Price Range (€) |
|---|---|
| Leather sandal | €20-40 |
| Leather loafer or flat | €35-65 |
| Leather boot (ankle) | €50-90 |
| Leather boot (knee/over-the-knee) | €70-130 |
| Leather sneaker | €40-80 |
| Mixed-material sneaker | €35-70 |

### Lead Times

| Stage | Duration | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Lasts creation (new shape) | 4-8 weeks | €300-800 per last |
| First sample | 4-6 weeks after last approval | — |
| Counter-sample / fit corrections | 2-4 weeks per round | — |
| Bulk production | 8-12 weeks after sample approval | — |
| **Total for new style** | **18-30 weeks** | — |
| **Adapting existing last** | **12-18 weeks** | — |

Sample cost: €80-300 per pair, plus last costs for new shapes.

> If planning a Spring/Summer launch, be in conversation with factories by the previous September at the latest.

## Key Trade Shows for Footwear & Knitwear

### Footwear
- **Micam** (Milan, Feb & Sep) — major international footwear trade show where Portuguese factories exhibit
- **Expo Riva Schuh** (Riva del Garda, Italy, Jan & Jun) — Sojor and others exhibit here
- **Lineapelle** (Milan) — leather and materials fair

- **Première Vision** (Paris, Feb & Sep) — global textile and fashion sourcing
- **ModaLisboa** (Lisbon, biannual) — Portuguese fashion week
- **Portugal Fashion** (Porto, biannual) — Portuguese fashion week
