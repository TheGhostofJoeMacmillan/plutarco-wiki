## Context: Portugal's Knit Fabric Industry

Portugal has been recognized by **Première Vision Paris** as "Europe's premier supplier for high-quality, innovative, and accessible knitwear." The northern region — specifically the **Ave and Cávado valleys (Braga, Barcelos, Santo Tirso, Guimarães)** — concentrates the country's knitting mills, with decades of circular-knitting expertise, highly technical machinery, and vertical integration from spinning to finishing.

**Key advantages over Italian mills:**
- **20-40% lower cost** at comparable or better quality (Eurostat/ATP data)
- **Lower MOQs** — Portuguese mills accept smaller runs (100-300m vs Italian 500m+ minimums)
- **Faster lead times** — 4-8 weeks vs 12-16 from Italy
- **Vertical integration** — spinning, knitting, dyeing, finishing all within 30km radius
- **Sustainability leadership** — GOTS, GRS, OEKO-TEX, B Corp certifications widely held
- **AI defect detection** — Smartex (Portuguese company) deployed in leading mills
- **Traceability** — compact, digitally monitored chains make fiber-to-fabric tracing operational

**What "higher than Italian quality" means in practice:**
Portugal excels at premium circular knits (jersey, interlock, rib, jacquard, fleece) — the fabric types used in high-end ready-to-wear, activewear, and luxury basics. Italy maintains an edge in ultra-luxury flat-bed knitwear (cashmere sweaters, fully-fashioned garments) and heritage woven textiles. For knit **fabrics** (not finished sweaters), Portuguese mills match or exceed Italian quality at lower cost.

**Première Vision highlights these Portuguese mill leaders:**
- **Sidónios** — flat-knit and finished goods
- **A. Sampaio & Filhos** — market leaders in sophisticated circular fabrics
- **Positive Materials (PDS Group)** — sustainability-driven innovation, collaborates with Spiber, Spinnova, Evrnu
- **Valerius 360°** — circular economy, textile waste → new textiles
- **Smartex** — AI defect detection technology
- **Casa da Malha** — elevated circular knits
- **Brito Knitting** — premium circular knit fabrics, Blossom Première Vision exhibitor
- **TINTEX Textiles** — naturally advanced knits, B Corp, 25+ years

## How to Approach These Mills as Maxine Agency

1. **Position Bea as a sourcing agent** connecting international brands with Portuguese mills — not a fabric buyer. The mills will understand this role.
2. **Request fabric swatch books / hangers** — mills send these free to established agents. This is the standard first step.
3. **Visit in person** — all mills are within 30-90 min of Porto. Book appointments via email first. Most have showrooms.
4. **Ask about their current collection** — most mills develop one-season-ahead collections that they present at trade shows. You can access these immediately by visiting.
5. **For custom fabric development** — bring the brand's tech pack or reference fabrics. Mills like TINTEX, Casa da Malha, and IRV develop bespoke fabrics to spec.
6. **MOQs for fabric** — typically 100-500kg per quality/color for stock fabrics. Custom development may require 300kg+ minimum. Confirm per mill.
7. **Lead times** — 3-6 weeks for stock fabrics, 6-10 weeks for custom development.
8. **Trade shows to meet all mills at once:** Première Vision Paris (Feb & Sep), Milano Unica (Feb & Sep). Portuguese mills exhibit heavily at both.
